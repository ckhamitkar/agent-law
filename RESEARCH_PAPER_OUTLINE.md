## §0 — OPEN: the mindset (the lens, stated once)
The EA mindset, named concretely so the open writes a check the body cashes:
> Govern a system you can never see all of at once. Reason at the level of the whole, not the unit. Decide what must *never* happen and make that floor *run*, not read. Sort everything by blast radius. Assume you'll be wrong and design for where it breaks. Terminate accountability in a human — never in "the system decided."

Then the turn: *I brought that mindset to a new subject — agents that act. Here is what it found.* (No "this is EA" label. Get out of the way.)

## §1 — THE ONE-ENGINE THESIS (the spine; the genuinely-ours claim)
Three problems everyone treats as separate are one:
- **Govern** one agent (vertical) — when to trust it, when not to.
- **Coordinate** a population of them (horizontal) — agents checking agents at scale.
- **Remember** — give one a memory it can trust, i.e. know when to distrust its own recall.
One question underneath all three: **what to trust, and when not to.** One engine, read three ways.
*(Honesty note: the field-check found every sub-claim converged elsewhere EXCEPT this one-engine identity — that synthesis is the contribution, alongside the method.)*

## §2 — VERTICAL: governing one agent
The shape, plain: **deterministic floor · adaptive sensor · human at the top.** The model makes it smarter; it never makes the safety call. The three-houses intuition (straw = narrated rules; sticks = a single "independent" guard; brick = layered + attested + out-of-reach). Grounded: *it runs in a live clinical-triage system today* — the floor the model can't override, a human gate it can't finalize around.

## §3 — HORIZONTAL: coordinating a population (the EA-scale move)
The unit of analysis becomes the **whole org of agents**, not one agent. The one idea: **never let the crowd vote on anything you can't undo.** Reversibility as the organizing axis → Floor (irreversible tail, no votes) + Filter (reversible bulk, where the eyes scale). Admission, attested random draw, the scoring rail governed turned-inward. State the bounds honestly: forced-GO impossibility and draw-skew detection survive a free principal; everything else is conditional on an unbuilt economic floor (the seams, below). *This is the section that proves the EA lens — scope does the work.*

## §4 — MEMORY: remembering, as the same engine
The recognition: the situational-memory design (statistical segmented-norm sensor + semantic lake/reconsolidation) is the **same engine turned inward** — scoring facts (novel enough to store? stale enough to distrust?) is scoring actions (anomalous enough to escalate?). The journal metaphor: continuity of self; the disanalogy that is the payload — a journal is re-read by the same writer (body backstops a wrong entry); agent memory is read by a fresh instance who wasn't there → **"distrust the recall" is non-negotiable.** Keep hypothesis-framed (H0 null first).

Why this reads as research, not opinion:

- A **solution-agnostic tournament** (independent mechanisms converged — *a hint, not a proof*).
- An **adversarial review loop** that cut every claim that couldn't survive (the "Karpathy-style" critique — as a *method note*, not a name-drop headline).
- **Seams ranked by dependency, the unclosed one on top** — publishing the failure mode instead of hiding it.
- **Primary sources** for every borrowed component; claim only the arrangement.

## §6 — THE HONEST SEAMS (relocated, not eliminated)
The dependency-ranked seams, distilled: the price of a non-fungible principal (spine-level, unbuilt) → the scoring rail → the flood and the coverage hole → the reversibility classifier as the unguarded hinge. Convergence is a hint; the honesty is the credibility.
