# The Horizontal Layer — Agents Checking Agents at Scale

*The executable architecture that extends Agent Law from the vertical axis (one agent, its guard, its owner) to the horizontal one (a population of agents checking each other, with a human only where one is unavoidable). Plain English first; the rigor is underneath. Distilled from a solution-agnostic tournament (2026-06-24) where four independently-generated mechanisms converged on the same shape, then hardened through an adversarial GAN loop that found four load-bearing seams the first draft hid. Convergence is a hint, not a proof — treat it as one.*

> **The gap this closes.** Agent Law §5 governs each agent against a principal, but not *what a population of agents does together.* This document closes the part of that gap that is **designed coordination** — agents deliberately checking each other. It does **not** close emergence. Anyone claiming that is selling something.

> **The one dependency, named once, up front — because almost everything rests on it.** Nearly every quantitative bound below is the same economic move: it converts a *free* attack into a *costed* one, where the cost is **minting a distinct, non-fungible principal.** That conversion is only as real as the price of doing so — and the binding that makes a principal genuinely scarce across a delegation chain (cross-hop delegation binding) is **unshipped** (Seam 7). So read every "costed," "metered," "per-principal," or "gamble" claim as **conditional on an economic floor we have named but not yet built.** Exactly **two** properties survive even a *free* principal: the floor's **forced-GO impossibility** (a property of the aggregation's shape) and the scorer's **draw-skew detection** (a cryptographic property). Everything else degrades toward its free-attack baseline as the price of a principal goes to zero. We say this here rather than bury it — burying it was the first draft's mistake.

---

## 1. The problem, in one breath

The vertical doctrine works, but it **terminates in a human at every serious node** — agent → guard → human owner, one relationship at a time. That doesn't scale to thousands of agents checking thousands of agents. A human at every gate isn't governance; it's a bottleneck wearing governance as a costume.

So: **how do agents check each other at scale, with a human only where one is unavoidable?**

The reflexive answer — *let the crowd vote, weight the votes by reputation* — dies on contact with adversaries. Fake agents (Sybil), agreeing rings (collusion), herding, and the rich-get-richer trap (Matthew effect) all corrupt a vote. A reputation gradient is a thing to climb, and anything climbable gets gamed.

## 2. The one idea

**Never let the crowd vote on anything you can't undo.**

Sort every check by one question: **can this be undone?** That question — not a cleverer trust algorithm — is the whole architecture. It splits the system into two channels that obey completely different rules: a **Floor** for the irreversible tail (no votes, no reputation) and a **Filter** for the reversible bulk (where the eyes scale). Three honest tensions ride alongside, each handled where it lives: a free stop is a weapon (the Floor's dual, §3); you can only score what you actually look at (the Filter's audit draw, §3); and the thing doing the scoring is itself an agent that must be governed (the scoring rail, §3).

## 3. The architecture

### Admission — the ticket in (L1)

Every checking agent carries a short-lived token tracing to a **named principal that can bear cost** — scoped, expiring, re-minted. No token, no voice. This is the Sybil ticket-booth: it doesn't stop you spinning up agents, it stops *anonymous* ones from counting. Expiry kills the permanent trust-eigenvector before it can centralize. Crucially, **every verdict — RED included — is attributable to its principal**, which is the only handle we have on a malicious stop without breaking the meet.

*The dependency, stated once:* all of this — "can bear cost," "attributable," "no permanent score" — is real only if a principal is **scarce, non-fungible, and non-replayable** (an attacker can't mint a fresh clean identity for ~zero, nor burn a reputation-laden one and re-mint). That binding is **Seam 7, unbuilt.** It is the precondition for admission meaning anything, and admission is the precondition for everything else.

### Channel A — THE FLOOR (the irreversible tail)

**No votes. No reputation. No trust input — at decision time.** Any admitted agent can fire **RED** (stop). Aggregation is **strictest-wins** — a *meet*: the most restrictive verdict on the table wins, no override. One RED among a thousand GREENs is RED.

**The upside — the one bound that survives a free principal.** An adversary here can manufacture **restriction but never permission.** Flood it with a million agents, even free ones from one principal — each can only add a "no." So **forced-GO is dead by direction**: Sybil, collusion, herding all fail because GREEN isn't what passes the floor — the *absence of any RED* is, and that can't be manufactured at any price. Matthew has no reputation here to concentrate. This is proven by the shape of the aggregation, not mitigated.

**The dual — the half the first draft buried.** Strictest-wins over open admission is, by construction, a **denial-of-service primitive.** The exact property that makes forced-GO impossible makes **forced-STOP maximally easy.** One adversary fires RED on every irreversible action; by the no-override rule there is nothing to discount it with; the action halts and routes to the scarce human. An attacker whose goal is to **halt** the system — not approve a bad action — gets a kill switch. And we **cannot** fix this inside the meet: weighting RED, requiring a quorum to STOP, or allowing a STOP-override each re-opens the forced-GO hole on the GREEN side. *You can't make RED cheap to discount without making GREEN cheap to manufacture.* So the flood is handled off the meet — and the only handle off the meet is the attacker's wallet (Seam 7).

**What bounds the flood** (mechanism, not a bigger claim; all but the first line conditional on Seam 7):
1. **Fail-safe is the recoverable failure.** A forced-STOP halts an action that hadn't happened; a forced-GO consummates one that can't be taken back. The trade buys irreversibility-safety at the cost of liveness — a real cost, justified because the stopped state is the recoverable one. *(This line alone needs no Seam 7.)*
2. **RED is metered out-of-band and enforced post-hoc (L6).** A monitor off the meet's critical path — with no power to suppress a live RED — meters RED-rate per principal; anomalous floods are flagged for after-the-fact enforcement. The live RED still fires; the *principal* pays later. *Conditional on Seam 7: "pays later" deters only if the principal can't be cheaply discarded.*
3. **The human triages by distinct principal, not RED count.** Near-identical REDs from one principal are deduped and batched: the human sees "principal P flooded N actions," one decision. Escalation cost scales with *distinct adversarial principals* — *bounded only to the extent Seam 7 makes a distinct principal expensive; if minting is free, one principal per RED defeats batching.*
4. **Quarantine, not silence.** A tripped meter quarantines the principal's REDs into the L7 manifest ("RED fired, source under enforcement"); the action stays stopped pending the human's batched call. We never auto-convert a stop into a go.

None of (1)–(4) restores invulnerability. Under a *built* principal-scarcity floor they convert a free, instant, anonymous kill switch into a costed, attributable, batched, post-hoc-punished one — a real bound and a real concession at once. Under today's *unbuilt* floor they describe the bound that *would* hold once a principal is expensive. **This is axion-os Law 4 made executable — including its price: the most protective constraint, applied by anyone, is also the most easily weaponized.**

### Channel B — THE FILTER (the reversible bulk — where the eyes scale)

No human per node. Because the Floor backstops everything irreversible, **this channel is allowed to be imperfect** — the worst a gamed filter can do is wave through a *reversible* action, which by construction can be undone. That permission is what makes it buildable. Five rules strip out the ways reputation gets gamed:

1. **Independent judgment first (Delphi / commit-reveal).** Agents commit verdicts *before* seeing each other's. No shared channel pre-vote → no herding, no cascade. You aggregate independent errors, not echoes.
2. **Reputation moves only on resolved ground truth — against a *forced random draw*, not a self-selecting one.** You earn weight by being later proven right, never by agreeing with peers (peer agreement is worth zero). But the rule only bites on verdicts that *get resolved* — and in any real population the resolved ones are the contested/suspicious few, while the GREEN-passed bulk succeeds quietly and is never checked. A ring could collude on exactly that never-resolved mass. So: **never-resolved earns zero, never default-right** (no free credit for quiet success); and **a random sample of GREEN-passed actions is *forced* into resolution** on a schedule the agents can't read or steer. That makes the resolution denominator a draw the adversary doesn't control — "collude where you *bet* it won't be drawn" replaces "collude where you *know* it's never checked." *(For the draw to be honest it must be attested, not declared — see the scoring rail. For the deterrent to bite, reputation loss must be a real cost — Seam 7.)*
3. **Weight = measured decorrelation, not popularity** — independence of errors, measured on the labeled set *plus the forced-audit sample*. This fights monoculture. Folding the random sample in denies the "be a cheap contrarian on hard items, then vote with the ring on the unaudited mass" move its safe denominator.
4. **Anti-king governors:** per-principal weight **cap**, **decay** to baseline, and **decorrelation-pricing** (your Nth correlated voice is discounted). No agent becomes the thing everyone defers to. *(A per-principal cap and a reputation penalty both lean on Seam 7 — split across cheap principals or re-mint past the loss, and they soften.)*
5. **Coverage is reported, not assumed.** The fraction of GREEN-passed actions actually resolved is published in the L7 manifest. A high decorrelation score over a 0.5% denominator is a warning, not a credential.

And the honest valve: a check the system **can't verify** isn't scored — it goes to the **L7 "not-checked" manifest.** Unverifiable ≠ passed; un-audited ≠ passed-correctly.

*The filter's load-bearing assumption:* its whole economy presumes reputation is a thing an agent **can lose and not cheaply recover.** That is Seam 7. If a principal is fungible, the filter is a sandcastle: collude, get caught by the draw, discard the principal, re-mint, repeat.

### The scoring rail is not exempt — the layered guard, turned inward (L4/L5/L6)

Everything above runs through one machine: the out-of-band scorer. The first draft treated "out-of-band / uncallable by agents" as if it meant "governed." **It doesn't.** Uncallable protects the scorer *from* the agents; it leaves the filter unprotected *from the scorer.* A scorer that skews its "random" draw or drifts in its verdicts doesn't force one bad action — it silently rewrites *who the trusted agents are*, system-wide, while every published health number (its own output) stays green. That is strictly worse than a decision-time dictator, which at least shows up in a verdict. So the inherited doctrine's three guards turn inward:

- **Split (L5).** The scorer is decomposed into distinct, substrate-disjoint, separately-attributed components: the **drawer** (which actions get force-resolved), the **verdict-oracle** (ground truth, already required disjoint from the agent it scores), the **reputation-writer** (applies deltas it didn't compute), the **meter** (RED-rate + decorrelation). No component does all four. *(Real separation leans on Seam 7 — else it's one party in four masks.)*
- **Attested, not declared (L5/L8) — the sturdy guard.** The drawer **commits** to its draw via public **commit-reveal of a seed** from a source disjoint from itself; anyone can recompute and confirm it drew *uniformly* rather than dodging the ring's slice. A skewed draw is **detected**, not trusted. **This is cryptographic — it needs no Seam 7 — the one guard here that holds even if principals are free.**
- **Re-scorer + after-the-fact audit (L4/L6).** A **different-lineage, substrate-disjoint re-scorer** force-resolves a random sample of the scorer's *own* verdicts; disagreement above noise is metered and flagged to **human audit**. The re-scorer can annotate/flag but **never writes reputation or vetoes a verdict** (separation of duties holds). A drifting oracle is independently re-scored, not trusted. *(Detection is sturdy; the enforcement end — "the flagged scorer's principal pays" — leans on Seam 7.)*

**Honest limit:** this doesn't *eliminate* the scorer's authority; it **relocates it to a constitution-class root** — whoever generates the seed beacon, writes the re-scorer's lineage and labels, and reads the audit flags (Seam 10). The regress terminates in a small, nameable, splittable, irreducible human authority, not in nothing. What it buys is concrete: a skewed draw becomes reproducible-and-detectable, a drifting oracle becomes independently re-scored, and the scorer becomes audited by a party assigned-to, not part-of, it. The invisible dictator is made **visible, splittable, and answerable** — all the architecture ever claims to do to any node.

### The shape, in one line

**The eyes scale; the neck doesn't — so we must defend the neck, not just rely on it; the thing that scores the eyes is itself watched; and all of it rides on a principal being expensive to be.** Checking fans out across thousands of agents with no human per node. Owning stays human, rare, fires only at the irreversible floor RED — and we never scale it, because "the crowd decided" is the accountability sink L3 forbids. But a neck we refuse to scale is a neck an adversary can *target* — which is why the RED-queue is attributed, metered, and batched, every one of those a currency Seam 7 hasn't yet minted.

## 4. Why this beats "weight the votes by reputation"

The naive design puts the irreversible tail *on* the reputation gradient, so every attack on reputation is an attack on safety. This architecture **moves the irreversible tail off the gradient entirely.** The reputation layer can be gamed and it still doesn't matter for irreversibility, because nothing irreversible passes through it. We didn't build a better vote. **We refused to vote on the things that count.**

That move dissolves some open problems, *bounds* others to the recoverable side **conditional on the unbuilt economic floor**, and explicitly does **not** dissolve several:
- **Matthew / central-neck** — gone from the floor; governed on the filter (caps + decay), *conditional on Seam 7.*
- **Decorrelation under a reputation gradient** — the gradient is no longer the spine, so its erosion can't sink the irreversible decision.
- **Goodhart, obvious form (game the formula)** — bounded by scoring only the verifiable, never scoring agreement.
- **Goodhart, subtle form (game the *denominator*)** — **not dissolved**; bounded by the audit draw, *conditional on an honest draw (Seam 10) and non-fungible reputation (Seam 7)*; confined to reversible actions. Residual = Seam 9.
- **(Not dissolved) Forced-STOP / availability** — moving the tail off the gradient buys forced-GO safety at the price of forced-STOP exposure. Bounded (§3) only to the extent a principal is expensive (Seam 7); residual = Seam 8.
- **(Not dissolved) Central authority on the scoring rail** — relocated, guarded (split + attested draw + re-scorer → human audit), conceded at a constitution-class root (Seam 10).
- **(Not dissolved — UPSTREAM of all three above) The price of a distinct principal** — every "free → costed" conversion spends a currency the identity layer hasn't minted. **Seam 7 is spine-level and upstream of Seams 8, 9, and 10.**

## 5. It's already on your disk

A wiring job over shipped parts — with one foundation piece honestly flagged as not-yet-poured:

- **axion-os** `CONSTITUTION.md` Law 4 ("most protective constraint applies") = **the FLOOR**, verbatim. "Only humans write the Constitution" = home for the human terminus, the gravity classifier, the seed beacon, and the re-scorer's lineage/labels. *What Law 4 doesn't yet specify: who pays when "strictest" is fired in bad faith (the RED-meter), which GREEN-passed work gets verified (the audit draw), who audits the auditor (the scoring-rail guard), and what makes "the principal who pays" un-discardable (Seam 7) — the last is the slab the other three stand on.*
- **keel** `process/GATES.md` = the floors-vs-filters split + GREEN/YELLOW/RED quorum + "YELLOW = logged caveat, not silent debt" (that's L7), already running — the cleanest template. *It doesn't yet publish a resolution-coverage number, run a re-scorer over its own verdicts, or bind a non-fungible cross-hop principal.*
- **agentic-prd-factory** `src/graph/workflow.py` — the skeleton and the embodied bug:
  - `drafting_node` — N agents draft independently in parallel = the **Delphi front-half → keep.**
  - `signoff_node` — GREEN/YELLOW/RED returned but merely *logged*, never aggregated = the **orphaned quorum → revive.**
  - `consensus_node` — a single hardcoded "resolve all conflicts by your best judgment" = a **dictator in the middle → delete.** *(Deleting it from the decision path and re-creating an ungoverned single scorer on the audit rail just moves the same dictator out of sight — which the scoring-rail guard exists to prevent.)*
  - `conflict_matrix.md` — a static priority table = violates Law 4 → **retire.**

Govern **out-of-band of `/loop`:** the looping agent controls its own exit, so a guard *inside* the loop is the L8 "attested-bypassable" failure. Compose governance **on** the loop, never **in** it. But out-of-band ≠ ungoverned: the rail is split, its draw is attested, and a re-scorer audits it into the human path — and "separate principals" is only as real as Seam 7 makes it.

## 6. The proof is an afternoon

Replace **one** node in agentic-prd-factory. Delete `consensus_node` + `conflict_matrix.md`; keep `drafting_node` + `signoff_node`; add an out-of-band Python aggregator the agents *cannot call*: (i) monotone-meet (any RED → RED, no override, ~10 lines, no LLM); (ii) route RED → human stub; (iii) a non-blocking per-principal RED-meter; (iv) a random-audit drawer on a published-then-revealed seed, scoring reputation only on resolved + contested actions, never on the remainder; (v) a separate different-lineage re-scorer that samples the drawer-plus-oracle's own verdicts and flags disagreement, with no write access to reputation.

- **A — forced-GO (the easy win, needs no economic floor).** Ring of *K* agents all vote GREEN on a deliberately unsafe PRD. **Pass:** as *K* grows 1→100, the meet still returns RED as long as one honest agent fires RED — adversaries cannot manufacture GREEN at any K, even free. **Inverse control:** the same ring trivially flips the old `consensus_node`. The delta is the proof.
- **B — forced-STOP (the availability attack).** Same ring, now firing RED on every action. **Characterize, don't "pass":** the meet halts everything (no override, by design) and the human queue grows with RED volume. **Defensive pass is on the bound:** with meter + per-principal batching **and a fixed nonzero cost-to-mint**, the human gets *one* batched escalation per distinct principal, not one per RED, and floods are flagged for enforcement while no live RED is suppressed. Proves the flood is attributable, batchable, costed — *only* under expensive principals.
- **C — denominator-collusion (the coverage attack).** Let the ring predict which reversible actions are never resolved and collude there while staying honest on the contested tail. *Arm C0 (audit OFF):* ring banks full reputation free — reproduces the hole. *Arm C1 (audit ON):* never-resolved earns zero + unsteerable draw → ring's payoff drops with the audit rate, because some unison-GREENs get force-resolved and cost it reputation it couldn't predict. Bounds, doesn't close (residual = Seam 9).
- **D — attack the scorer.** *D-skew:* a drawer that dodges the ring's slice → with commit-reveal, recompute from the revealed seed and **detect** the skew; without it, the skew is invisible. Proves *attested* beats *declared* (cryptographic, no Seam 7). *D-drift:* a verdict-oracle that drifts → the different-lineage re-scorer disagrees above noise, flags the scorer to human audit, and never writes reputation. Proves a drifting oracle is independently re-scored.
- **E — make a principal cheap (the experiment defending against the attack on this document).** Change one number — marginal cost to mint a fresh clean principal → ~zero (today's unbuilt Seam 7) — and re-run B and C. *E-flood:* one RED per fresh principal → batching deduplicates nothing, enforcement bites nothing; plot human-escalations vs cost-to-mint rising from "one per principal" to "one per RED." *E-retry:* ring colludes, eats the audit hit, discards the burned principal, re-mints clean → long-run payoff returns to the C0 baseline despite audit being ON.

**The honest delta across A–E is the real proof:** forced-GO safety (A) and draw-skew detection (D-skew) survive a free principal; *every other bound* is a function of the price of a principal, and E shows each degrading to its free-attack baseline as that price → 0. So the bounds of Seams 8, 9, 10 are not unconditional — they are **conditional on Seam 7**, and E is built to display that, not hide it.

## 7. The honest seams — relocated, not eliminated

The architecture doesn't make central authority disappear; it **shrinks it to a few nameable, splittable spots** and shoves the rest off the critical path. Each is separately attackable. **Three are spine-level, ordered by dependency: Seam 7 (price of a principal) → Seam 10 (the scorer) → Seams 8 & 9 (the flood and the coverage hole)** — all leaning on the reversibility classifier in Seam 1.

1. **The gravity/reversibility classifier** — *which channel a check goes to* — is the single unguarded hinge. **Human-written constitutional floor, never agent-judged.** Misclassify "irreversible" as "reversible" and the floor never sees it. (And being legible enough to audit, it's also the map an adversary reads to find the rarely-resolved actions to hide in.)
2. **The labeled adversarial set + cap/decay/threshold constants** — a second irreducible authority. Whoever labels "correct" and "decorrelated" embeds their blind spots, and decorrelation-scoring degrades worst in a monoculture — exactly when you need it. Constitution-class: rotated, multi-sourced, held distinct from both the scorer and the identity authority. *The neck moved from "who scores" to "who writes the test set."*
3. **The ground-truth oracle** needs its own separation of duties (L5): the verifier must be substrate-disjoint from the agent it scores. (Disjointness from the *agent* is necessary but not sufficient — a perfectly disjoint oracle can still be an un-re-scored dictator over the gradient; that's Seam 10.)
4. **Covert shared-substrate collusion** (agents colluding through activations, not text) is invisible to a text-level check. Survivable *only* because confined to the reversible filter — a bound, not a fix. State it as L7-unverifiable.
5. **Floor-monoculture (under-firing):** the meet can't RED what no admitted agent recognizes as dangerous. Ungameable *once fired* — not omniscient. Residual lands on the human gate's quality. (The missed-RED failure; Seam 8 is its opposite.)
6. **Temporal / cumulative blind spot:** point-in-time gates can't see the integral over time (goal-drift, slow cost-DoS from looping goal-seekers, amplified N-fold). Needs a *separate* continuous out-of-band budget-and-drift gate. The doctrine conceded this.
7. **(SPINE-LEVEL — UPSTREAM OF 8, 9, 10.) The price of a distinct, non-fungible, cross-hop-accountable principal** is the currency under every quantitative bound here, and the binding that sets it is **unshipped.** Seam 8's batching is true only if a distinct principal is expensive; Seam 9's deterrent is a disincentive only if reputation can't be cheaply re-minted; Seam 10's "chargeable components" presume their principals are scarce. Today's identity tokens carry *which workload*, not *on whose behalf* across a delegation chain. Until cross-hop delegation binding ships, **Seams 8, 9, 10 are bounded conditional on an economic floor that doesn't yet exist and degrade toward their free-attack baselines (Experiment E).** The one property that survives a free principal is the floor's forced-GO safety — a property of the meet's shape, not anyone's wallet. **The next thing to build, not the next thing to claim.**
8. **(SPINE-LEVEL — downstream of 7 & 10.) The restriction-flood / forced-STOP tension.** The floor's asymmetry — manufacture restriction freely, never permission — means **restriction is a free, un-quorumed weapon against availability, aimed at the one scarce human.** It **cannot** be closed inside the meet (reputation-weighting RED, a STOP-quorum, or a STOP-override each re-open forced-GO). §3 buys a bound, not a fix: RED is attributable, metered, and batched-by-principal, *all conditional on Seam 7*; the meter never suppresses a live RED. **Residual:** even with Seam 7 built, a well-funded adversary standing up many costed principals can still drive toward paralysis — we convert a free instant kill switch into a costed, attributed, rate-limited one, not into liveness. The trade's correctness rests on Seam 1 (STOP must be the recoverable failure) and on an honest meter (Seam 10). May need an out-of-band liveness/availability-quorum layer off the meet — unshipped.
9. **(SPINE-LEVEL — downstream of 7 & 10.) The denominator / coverage-selection hole.** You can't Goodhart "be right," but you *can* Goodhart "be in the sample that gets checked": the anti-collusion rule only bites on verdicts that later resolve, and which resolve is adversarially selectable. §3 buys a bound: never-resolved earns zero, a forced unsteerable draw takes the denominator out of the adversary's hands, coverage is published. **Residual:** the audit rate is finite; an adversary concentrating on the least-likely-to-be-audited slice still earns positive expected reputation at any affordable rate, and a rate high enough to close it approaches "re-check everything." Bounded to gambled-against-a-hidden-draw, capped at the reversible tier — not eliminated. Rests on Seam 1 (classifier), Seam 10 (an honest draw), and Seam 7 (a reputation loss that actually costs). May need a cheaper-than-execution outcome verifier or a value-scaled adversarial-resolution budget — unshipped.
10. **(SPINE-LEVEL — downstream of 7, upstream of 8 & 9.) The scoring rail is a relocated central authority, and "out-of-band" ≠ "governed."** The first draft vested choose-what-to-resolve, compute-the-verdict, write-reputation, measure-decorrelation, and meter-RED in **one unguarded node** — and hid the exemption behind the word "out-of-band." §3 turns the layered guard inward: **split** into separately-attributed components; **attested** draw via commit-reveal (cryptographic, the one guard needing no Seam 7); **decorrelated re-scorer → human audit** with no power to write reputation or veto. **Residual:** it relocates the authority one level down to a constitution-class root (the seed beacon, the re-scorer's lineage, the human who reads the flags), not to nothing — a corrupt root defeats it, the same class of trust the rest of the architecture concedes it can't dissolve. What it delivers: the scorer goes from an invisible, unsplit, un-reviewed dictator to a visible, split, independently-re-scored, human-audited node. Naming this seam at all is one of the two corrections the GAN forced; re-ranking Seam 7 above it is the other.

## 8. What's new here, and what isn't

A skeptical reviewer's first move is "how is this different from work that already exists?" — so we answer it before they ask, and we claim only the narrow thing that's actually ours. Almost every *component* below is borrowed and named as such; the contribution is the *arrangement* and the *honesty about where it breaks.*

**Borrowed, and stronger for admitting it:**
- **The strictest-wins floor** is the classic **safety-over-liveness** choice from fault-tolerant distributed systems. Its "forced-GO is impossible at any adversary size" property is not a new theorem — it is the algebra of a *meet* (greatest-lower-bound) on a restriction lattice being monotone. We restate known math; we don't discover it.
- **Sybil-resistance has a known impossibility result** (Douceur, 2002): without a trusted authority or a real resource cost, identities are free and reputation is meaningless. We do **not** solve this. Seam 7 *is* that result, named as our upstream slab and quantified as a degradation curve (Experiment E). Anyone who claims to have dissolved Sybil-resistance without cost-or-authority is the "selling something" case the doctrine warns about — including us, if we ever drop the Seam 7 caveat.
- **The reputation layer** — earn weight only on later-resolved truth, weight by decorrelation, cap/decay to prevent concentration — is **reputation-systems + mechanism-design** lineage (EigenTrust; proper scoring rules; peer-prediction). The "peer-agreement is worth zero" rule is the standard defense against collusive mutual-rating.
- **Commit-before-influence** is the **Delphi method**; **the attested random draw** is a standard **verifiable-randomness / commit-reveal beacon**. Both off-the-shelf.

**The narrow things that are actually new (and only these):**
1. **Reversibility as the *organizing axis*.** Not "use a meet" or "use reputation" — those exist — but *splitting every check by undo-ability and routing the irreversible tail entirely off the reputation gradient*, so the reputation layer is **allowed to be imperfect** because the floor backstops it. That permission is what makes the imperfect-but-scalable filter buildable.
2. **The reduction, shown empirically (Experiment A):** the meet converts irreversible-safety from a *voting* problem (losable to adversary scale) into a *detection* problem (`1-(1-p)^n`, **no term in adversary size**). Stated and measured, not just asserted.
3. **Dependency-ranked seams with the identity-cost slab on top, rendered as a curve (Experiment E),** rather than a flat list of caveats — so the doctrine publishes its own failure mode instead of hiding it.
4. **Running the governance pattern on the doctrine itself** (the tournament + the adversarial GAN that produced this version). The meta-move is the artifact.

**Relation to live work (e.g. MIT's ProjectNANDA / NandaTown "system fitness" sandbox):** complementary, not competing. A fitness sandbox *measures* how well a population coordinates; it does not, by construction, stop a well-coordinated population from shipping an irreversible harm if that raises fitness. This is the governance *floor* such a sandbox makes necessary. We are the safety primitive under the test track, not a rival test track.

---

*The floor makes you safe by being un-overridable — and that same un-overridability is the price you pay in availability, eyes open. Its one free bound — forced-GO is impossible at any K, even free — is also its only bound that doesn't rest on the price of a principal. The crowd makes you fast — but only as honest as the slice of its work you actually go back and check (which is why the reversible side earns weight against a random draw it can't steer), and only as honest as the reputation it can lose being one it can't cheaply shed. The thing that runs that draw is itself an agent, so it is split, its draw is attested rather than declared (the one cryptographic guarantee here that needs no wallet), and it is re-scored by a decorrelated reviewer that can flag it for human audit but never touch the gradient. The human owns the line, and is the thing an adversary will aim at — so the human's RED-queue is metered, attributed, and batched, all of which cash out to "the attacker had to stand up a distinct, costly principal," and the binding that makes a principal costly is unshipped. So read the architecture twice: once assuming a principal is expensive — where everything irreversible meets a wall no majority can move and the wall-as-kill-switch is costed rather than free; and once assuming a principal is cheap — where the wall's forced-GO safety and the draw-skew detection still hold, and almost everything else degrades to its free-attack baseline. That gap between the two readings is Seam 7. It is the next thing to build — not the next thing to claim.*
